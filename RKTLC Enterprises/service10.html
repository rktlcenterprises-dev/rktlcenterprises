SEZ Scheme

The Special Economic Zone (SEZ) Scheme is designed to promote exports, boost foreign investment, and enhance competitiveness of Indian businesses. SEZs are specially designated duty-free enclaves considered to be outside the customs territory of India for trade operations, duties, and tariffs.

Why SEZ Scheme is Important?

  • Provides tax and duty benefits for companies operating in SEZs.
  • Encourages foreign direct investment and boosts exports.
  • Ensures world-class infrastructure and simplified procedures.
  • Supports employment generation and regional economic growth.

Key Benefits of SEZ Units

  • 100% Income Tax exemption on export income for the first 5 years, with reduced benefits thereafter.
  • Exemption from customs duty, GST, and central excise on imports/procurements.
  • Single-window clearance and simplified compliance.
  • Exemption from minimum alternate tax (MAT) and dividend distribution tax (DDT) in certain cases.
  • Permission for external commercial borrowings up to a prescribed limit.

Eligibility for Setting up an SEZ Unit

  • Export-oriented businesses in manufacturing, services, IT/ITES, biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, etc.
  • Approval required from the Development Commissioner of the SEZ.
  • Proposal must meet minimum area and investment criteria as per SEZ rules.

Required Documents

  • Application form in prescribed format to the Development Commissioner.
  • Company registration documents (MoA, AoA, Incorporation Certificate).
  • Project report and investment details.
  • Import Export Code (IEC) and GST registration.
  • Board resolution and authorization documents.

Key Tips

  • Evaluate long-term tax benefits before opting for SEZ status.
  • Ensure strict compliance with SEZ Act and Rules to avoid penalties.
  • Maintain separate accounts for SEZ transactions for audit purposes.
  • Regularly track DGFT and SEZ authority updates for policy changes.
